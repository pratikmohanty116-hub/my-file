05 — Key Insights 2025

What the Data Tells Us

01 · Climate
The End of Seasonal Predictability
India's traditional three-season structure — wet, cool, hot — has collapsed. Cold waves now begin in 13 states simultaneously instead of 2. Heatwaves strike in February. Floods and drought can coexist in the same month across different regions.
01
02 · Geology
80% of the Wealth is Still Underground
Only 20% of India's geological potential has been explored. With 163.9 MT copper reserves, 44.9 MT cobalt ore, and the world's largest thorium deposits, unlocking this wealth could transform India's position in the global critical-minerals race.
02
03 · Economy
Climate Risk Meets Economic Growth
India's 6.5% GDP growth story runs headlong into a climate crisis. Drought-hit agricultural states, flood-damaged infrastructure, and groundwater depletion in key economic zones threaten to erode the gains from manufacturing and renewable energy expansion.
03
04 · Energy
Renewable Momentum vs Coal Reality
Renewable output rose 17.2% while coal dependence fell 9.5% in 2025. Yet coal still dominates India's energy mix. The PLI scheme for specialty steel and $99B construction equipment market signals a demand surge that renewable supply must race to meet.
04
05 · Water
The Groundwater Crisis
Rajasthan alone extracts 16.74 billion cubic metres of groundwater annually. Drought-hit regions face severe depletion while flood regions battle contamination. Nearly half of seasonal rainfall now falls within just 20–30 hours, creating dangerous dry gaps between extreme events.
05
06 · Future
Critical Minerals & the Green Race
India signed agreements with Argentina for lithium, Australia for critical minerals, and Côte d'Ivoire for geological cooperation in 2024–25. With offshore mineral blocks now being auctioned for the first time, India is positioning itself as a global critical-minerals hub for the energy transition era.
